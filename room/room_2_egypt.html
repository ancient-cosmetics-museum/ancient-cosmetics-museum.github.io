Natron

Natron was used as basis for cremes. It was also used in the process of mummification, food preservation and soap creation.

Lotus

Perfumes were significant for burials due to the fact that lotus was a key ingredient in most fragrances, and lotus was said to be associated with purification and rejuvenation.

Myrrh

Myrrh was one of the most preferred scents for the creation of perfumes in Egypt. The most popular and best-known perfume was kyphi. It was made of frankincense, myrrh, mastic, pine resin, cinnamon, cardamom, saffron, juniper, mint, and other herbs and spices.

Terebinth

In ancient Egypt terebinth-resin was used on different body parts (wounds, eyes) for disinfection or purification, but also for its conserving qualities to prepare mummies for eternal life. Even though the tree doesn’t grow there naturally and the resin had to be imported.

Castor oil

Castor oil is a natural plant oil obtained from the seed of the castor plant. According to the Ebers Papyrus, an Egyptian medical text from 1500 b.c., Egyptian doctors used castor oil to protect the eyes from irritation. Moreover it was used as oil to soften skin from people of lower classes.
